Why This Is The Flagship

The Contract Intelligence Engine has the most powerful sales proposition of any module across all three verticals: "We typically find more in missed rebates and unclaimed credits in your first 90 days than our entire annual platform fee." That is almost impossible to say no to. Healthcare logistics operators hold complex portfolios of supplier contracts, NHS framework agreements, and commissioner contracts — most of which were negotiated by someone who has since left, have auto-renewal clauses nobody is monitoring, contain rebate structures nobody is tracking, and include performance obligations nobody is systematically measuring. The Contract Intelligence Engine reads every contract, tracks every obligation, and finds every penny that's owed but not being claimed. Mum's insider knowledge of how these contracts work in practice — including the ones NRS held — makes this product genuinely credible in the market.

The 5 Core Capabilities
  • Missed Rebate Detection — pharmaceutical and medical device suppliers offer volume-based rebates through NHS frameworks and direct supply agreements. Most operators don't track whether they've hit the volume thresholds that trigger them. The engine reads every contract, monitors purchase volumes against thresholds, and calculates exactly what rebates have been earned but not yet claimed. Most operators find 3–8% of their total procurement spend sitting in unclaimed rebates.
  • Auto-Renewal and Term Trap Monitoring — flags every contract with an auto-renewal clause 90, 60, and 30 days before the renewal date. Shows the renewal terms, the notice period required to renegotiate or exit, and whether the contract terms are above or below current market benchmarks. Prevents the "we meant to renegotiate that but it rolled over again" conversation.
  • Performance Bonus Identification — many NHS and commissioner contracts include performance clauses that entitle the operator to bonuses for exceeding KPIs. Most operators don't systematically track whether they've earned these. The engine reads the contract, monitors the relevant KPIs from operational data, and flags when a bonus is owed but hasn't been claimed.
  • Stock Audit and Valuation — automated stock audit against contracted minimum and maximum holding levels. Identifies stock above contracted maximum (potential return or credit opportunity), stock below contracted minimum (compliance risk), and slow-moving stock at risk of expiry write-off. Connects to procurement data to calculate the cash value of each finding.
  • Contract Compliance Risk Register — maps every obligation in every contract to an evidence source and compliance status. Red/Amber/Green rating for each contract. Flags where the organisation may be in technical breach before it becomes a formal issue — giving time to remedy rather than respond to a notice.

Section 00 — Product Overview
HealthOS:
The White Space Opportunity

Healthcare logistics and supply chain operators manage some of the most complex, high-stakes, and compliance-heavy operations in any industry — almost entirely without AI assistance. This is not a crowded market being disrupted. It is an untouched market waiting for its first intelligent operating system.

The Core Problem

Healthcare logistics and supply chain operators are drowning in compliance, workforce management, and procurement complexity — all managed manually. CQC inspections, staff certification tracking, agency staff procurement, medical supply chain risk, and shift scheduling are universal pain points. None of them have an AI system helping. The first platform that addresses all of these coherently — built by someone who has lived inside this sector — wins.

The Unfair Advantage

An HR Director who has worked inside a healthcare logistics and supply chain company (NRS) knows how these organisations make decisions, what the compliance pressures actually feel like, who the real buyers are, and what the daily operational pain points are — not from research, but from lived experience. NRS going into liquidation also means she saw operational failure up close — and knows exactly what the absence of good systems costs.
