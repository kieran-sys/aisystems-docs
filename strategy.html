"The highest-margin AI companies sell outcomes, not software. The highest-defensibility AI companies embed themselves so deeply into operations that switching cost becomes structural."

The following is a step-by-step, first-principles GTM plan for building the first vertical AI company. GTM Framework · AI Systems Co.
Phase-by-Phase Execution Plan
Phase 1 · Months 1–3
Design the MVP AI System
  • Identify the single most expensive problem in the chosen vertical. For dental: patient reactivation and new patient acquisition — a practice losing £80k/yr to lapsed patients and poor marketing ROI is the ideal prospect.
  • Build the MVP AI system focused on that one problem. Do not build the full OS. Build the module that generates the most measurable, fastest ROI. For dental: the AI Patient Reactivation + Marketing Attribution engine.
  • Instrument every element from Day 1 — the system must capture data that compounds value. Every patient interaction, every marketing touchpoint, every conversion event must be logged and stored.
  • Set up the core tech stack: Next.js frontend, Neon PostgreSQL, Claude API (Anthropic), Twilio (SMS/WhatsApp), Make.com for automation, GHL for CRM. Total build cost with AI assistance: £5k–£20k.
  • Define your outcome metrics: new patient bookings generated, reactivation rate, appointment fill rate, marketing cost per new patient. These become the product's core value proposition and pricing anchor.
Phase 2 · Months 2–4
Sign the First 3–5 Paying Clients
  • Leverage existing relationships. If you have dental clients already, approach them first — a warm introduction from a trusted partner converts 10x better than cold outreach. Offer a 60-day pilot at a discounted rate in exchange for data access and a case study commitment.
  • Price to close at this stage. The goal is data and proof, not margin. £500–£800/month is a psychological threshold most dental practice owners will not blink at — it's less than they spend on a single Google Ads day.
  • Do everything manually that the AI system doesn't yet automate. The goal is to prove the outcome, not the technology. Be prepared to do 20% of the work manually behind the scenes if needed to deliver the result.
  • Document every single outcome obsessively: before and after metrics, revenue recovered, time saved, DNAs reduced. These numbers will become the sales tool that closes the next 50 clients.
  • Get written testimonials, video walkthroughs, and ideally LinkedIn endorsements from early clients. Social proof in a tight-knit industry like dentistry spreads faster than any ad campaign.
Phase 3 · Months 4–8
Productise and Scale to 20–50 Clients
  • Systemise the onboarding and deployment process. Every client should be live within 5–7 business days. Build onboarding checklists, data connection guides, and automated setup flows that reduce manual delivery time.
  • Build the sales process around the ROI calculator. "Our average dental client recovers £42,000 in year one from lapsed patient reactivation alone. At £800/month, that's a 4.4x return in year one." This closes deals.
  • Activate referral channels: dental consultants, accountants who serve dentists, dental suppliers, and dental association relationships. A 15–20% referral commission on first-year revenue creates powerful distribution.
  • Begin speaking and publishing. Write monthly intelligence reports on dental marketing performance benchmarks. Post LinkedIn content using anonymised benchmark data. Become the industry's intelligence authority — not just a software vendor.
  • Start building the premium tier. Identify your top 5 clients and offer them the first version of the AI Copilot (fully agentic, multi-module). Charge £1,500–£2,000/month. Use this to fund the next development sprint.
  • Target your first DSO. One 10-site dental group at £800/month per site = £8,000 MRR from a single client. DSO operators are motivated by operational efficiency at scale — the value proposition is even stronger for groups.
Phase 4 · Months 8–18
Build the Moat — Data, Integrations, Depth
  • By month 8, you should have 20–50 clients generating real operational data. Begin building the vertical data intelligence layer — aggregate benchmarks, pattern recognition across practices, and AI models trained on your dataset. This is your competitive moat and cannot be replicated without the data.
  • Build the platform integrations that increase switching cost. Deep integrations with Exact, Dentally, SOE, and the major UK PMS providers mean that switching away from your platform requires migrating 12 months of connected data history — almost nobody will.
  • Launch the vertical benchmark report. "UK Private Dental Practice Performance Report — Q1 2026." This is published freely to the industry. It uses your anonymised data to show industry benchmarks. It positions your company as the industry intelligence authority and drives inbound leads at zero marginal cost.
  • Pursue dental industry association partnerships. A partnership with BDIA, BDA, or NASDAL endorsing your platform as the recommended AI marketing intelligence system delivers instant credibility and a distribution channel to thousands of practices.
  • Hire your first sales person — a dental industry insider who can open doors with practices through relationships, not cold outbound. Target someone who has previously sold into dental practices and comes with a Rolodex.
Phase 5 · Year 2
£1M ARR and Vertical 2 Launch
  • Target: 100–150 dental clients at an average of £900/month = £108k–£135k MRR / £1.3–£1.6M ARR. This is achievable with a 3-person team (founder, sales, customer success) supported by AI-automated delivery.
  • Launch the full DentalOS product at the top of the pricing stack. Existing clients are upsold; new DSO clients enter at this tier. Target 15–20 DSOs or group operators on the premium tier at £3k–£8k/month.
  • Begin vertical 2 using shared infrastructure. The legal services or construction sector is recommended as the second vertical — different buyer personas from dental, but using identical underlying technology. The second vertical should reach profitability 2x faster than the first.
  • Begin raising a seed round if capital is needed to accelerate, or run capital-efficiently if cash flow supports growth. At £1M+ ARR with 85%+ gross margin and strong retention, you are a fundable company at a strong valuation.
  • Define the exit strategy — either build toward a strategic acquisition (most likely exit by Year 5–7 to a Salesforce Health Cloud, Carestream, Henry Schein, or dental group) or continue compounding toward a platform company worth £50M–£200M+.
