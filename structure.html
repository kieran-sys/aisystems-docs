Team Structure Proposal · AI Systems Co.

A Different
Kind of Team

Everyone is becoming an expert at something. The people who matter most to what we're building are not hired employees — they're co-owners of something they helped build. This is a model for bringing the right people in, at the right level, in the right way.

Section 01 · The New Model
Why Traditional Hiring Fails

Most companies get funding, hire a team, and hope it works. The team is expensive, misaligned, and replaceable. We are doing this differently. Every person who joins this company early owns a piece of what they're building. The more you put in, the more you own. Simple. Defensible. Motivating.

The Old Way
  • Get funding. Burn it on salaries. Hope for revenue before cash runs out.
  • Hire based on CV. Find out after 6 months they don't actually care.
  • Everyone gets a salary. Nobody feels like an owner. Decisions take forever.
  • Fire people. When funding runs out or pivots happen, the team dissolves.
  • Founders own everything. Late employees have no stake. No real alignment.
  • The people who built it aren't the ones who benefit from what it becomes.
Our Way
  • Revenue before hiring. Stage 1 clients fund Stage 2. No burn rate panic.
  • Invite people we know. Trust already exists. Values already aligned.
  • Everyone owns equity. What you put in determines what you own. Skin in the game.
  • Flexible commitment levels. Full-time, part-time, or project-based — all valued differently.
  • Equity reflects contribution. Early, deep commitment = larger ownership. Fair by design.
  • The people in this room are the people who will be in the documentary. We're filming it.
The Core Principle

AI is making individual contributors exponentially more powerful. A team of 10 aligned, equity-holding, deeply motivated people built around AI tools can outperform a team of 100 conventional employees. We don't need 100 people. We need the right 25 — at different levels of commitment — all pulling in the same direction because they own what they're building.

Section 02 · The Three Tiers
Where Do You Sit?

There is no wrong tier. There is only the honest tier. The tier that reflects how much of yourself you can genuinely give to this right now. The tiers are not permanent. People move. Life changes. The structure accommodates that.

This is your primary commitment. You're building this every day. Decisions go through you. You are the company in its earliest form.

  • Vertical or function ownership
  • First access to salary as revenue grows
  • Largest equity allocation

The Most Important Thing About Equity

These numbers are not hypothetical motivational fodder. They are the logical outcome of the model if we execute it. The equity structure is designed to be fair, transparent, and motivating from Day 1. Nobody in this building is going to feel like they were used. Everyone who builds something here owns something here. That's not a policy. That's the point.
