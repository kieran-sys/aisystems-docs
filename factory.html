Section 00 — Product Overview
FactoryOS:
The Market Opportunity

Mid-market manufacturing is the largest addressable market of all three verticals — $16T globally — and the most data-rich industry on earth. Machine sensors, ERP systems, procurement databases, and energy meters generate millions of data points daily. Almost none of it is being analysed intelligently. That is the opportunity.

The Core Problem We're Solving

The average mid-market manufacturer (£5M–£500M revenue) has a modern ERP system, sophisticated machinery, and years of operational data — but zero AI intelligence layer sitting on top of it. Decisions about production scheduling, procurement timing, maintenance windows, and quality standards are made by experienced people using gut feel and spreadsheets. FactoryOS replaces gut feel with real-time AI intelligence — predicting failures before they happen, optimising decisions before they're made, and automating workflows before staff waste time on them.

Why Si Is The Unfair Advantage

30 years at Operations Director level in manufacturing means Si is the buyer persona. He understands the language, the politics, the procurement process, and the specific problems that keep ops directors awake at night. He can walk into any manufacturing boardroom as a peer — not a salesperson. His network of former colleagues, suppliers, and industry contacts represents a warm pipeline that would take a normal software company years and millions in marketing to replicate. The audit is Si's sales process. He walks in as a consultant and walks out with a contract.

The 6 Daily Deliverables
  • Factory Health Score Overnight — what happened on the night shift. Any machine anomaly readings, any quality flags triggered by the vision system, any energy spikes outside normal parameters. A single score (0–100) with a plain-English summary of what drove it. Takes 10 seconds to read. Replaces a 40-page shift report nobody reads.
  • Today's Risk Register — Top 3 — the three things most likely to cause a problem today. Machine with elevated vibration that's trending toward a maintenance alert. Supplier delivery running 2 days late that threatens tomorrow's production run. Operator on their 5th consecutive shift approaching WTR overtime limits. Ranked by financial impact if the risk materialises.
  • Cost Leakage Report — This Week — a weekly rolling view of where money is leaving the factory that shouldn't be. Energy waste by machine vs baseline. Procurement prices above current market rate. Overtime patterns that suggest scheduling inefficiency. Scrap rate by production line vs target. Every item has a £ figure next to it. Nothing abstract.
  • Production Performance vs Plan — how yesterday tracked against the production schedule. Where the gaps were, what caused them, and what today needs to look like to stay on track for the week's output commitments. Customer delivery risk flags if any orders are threatened.
  • Market Intelligence for Ops — commodity price movements relevant to the client's key materials (steel, copper, plastics, packaging — configured per client). Any relevant regulatory or standards changes. Regional skills market movements. Keeps the ops director commercially aware without them having to go looking.
  • One Improvement Opportunity — AI-identified highest-impact operational improvement available right now. Could be a load-shift scheduling change that saves £800 this week. A procurement substitution that saves £2,000 per month. A production sequencing change that recovers 2 OEE percentage points. One specific, actionable recommendation per day.

Core Features
  • Historical demand analysis — ingests 24+ months of order/shipment history from ERP export, identifies seasonal patterns, trends, and cyclical demand shifts
  • 30/60/90-day rolling forecast — generates forward-looking demand predictions by product family, SKU, or customer segment with confidence intervals
  • External signal integration — layers in economic indicators, commodity price movements, and industry indices to enhance forecast accuracy beyond historical patterns
  • Customer demand signals — if client shares customer order forecasts, integrates these as leading indicators to sharpen near-term predictions
  • Forecast vs actual tracking — compares each forecast to eventual actual demand, calculates MAPE (accuracy score), and improves model parameters over time
  • Production planning output — translates demand forecast directly into recommended production volumes, sequencing suggestions, and raw material requirements
  • Exception alerting — flags when actual demand diverges significantly from forecast, triggering production and procurement review
  • Weekly forecast briefing — auto-generated email summary of the week's demand outlook with key changes highlighted, sent to production planning team

Core Features
  • Dynamic safety stock calculation — replaces static safety stock rules with AI-calculated levels that account for demand variability and supplier lead time uncertainty per SKU
  • Reorder point optimisation — calculates optimal reorder point for each SKU based on demand forecast, supplier lead times, and target service level
  • Overstock identification — flags SKUs where current stock significantly exceeds optimal level, calculating the cash tied up in excess stock
  • Stockout risk scoring — risk-scores every SKU by probability of stockout in next 30/60/90 days given current stock and forecast demand
  • Automated reorder alerts — triggers purchase requisition alerts when stock approaches reorder point, with suggested order quantity and recommended supplier
  • ABC/XYZ classification — automatically classifies all SKUs by value and demand variability, enabling differentiated management strategies
  • Inventory health dashboard — visual overview of portfolio health: overstocked, optimal, understocked, and at-risk SKUs with financial values
  • Write-off risk prediction — identifies slow-moving stock at risk of obsolescence before it becomes a write-off problem
